I would call meditation my hobby

I know it sounds funny to call mediation a hobby but to be honest this was the first thing that came to my mind when thinking about what I frequently do in my freetime. I guess I wanted to share my passion about this simple habit that have literally changed my life.

What is meditation?

Meditation is a set of techniques that are intended to increase the state of awareness and focused attention. It is also a technique to change your consiousness. Meditation has a long history and even though it doesn't have to be a religious thing, almost every religion practices some type of meditation. Interested about it's history? Read more about it here.

Practicing meditation has shown to have many benefits on psychological wellbeing... more about that in the next chapter.

Benefits of meditation?

In addition to the psychological side, practicing mediation is proven to have various healt benefits too. Here is what you can gain when investing your time in meditation:

  • Reduce your stress levels
  • Control possible anxiety
  • Improve your self-image and positive outlook on life
  • Enhance your self-awareness
  • Increase the strength of your attention
  • Improve your sleep
  • Help to control pain

Myths about meditation

There is many myths about meditation that might stop you for even trying. To make your path easier I would like to bust some of the most common ones:

Myth #1: Your mind has to be quiet

...Meaning you shouldn't have any thoughts going on in your head. During your meditation practice it is not about wether you have thoughts or not, but more about just being aware that you have them, whithout analyzing or getting attached with them. You can just sit back and notice your thougths come and go.

Myth #2: You need to sit still while meditating

You might think that the only way to get into a meditative state is to sit down in a lotus position. That's not quite rigth, actually you can even meditate while hiking or walking in the nature.

Myth #3: Meditation takes hours

You don't need to sit down(or walk) for an hour to benefit from meditation, even 5 minute break will count.
